# ELO DOCUMENT 19: CROSS-DOMAIN LEARNING TRANSFER PROTOCOL
# Enterprise Learning Operations — How Knowledge Flows Between Domains

---

## Purpose
Enable horizontal knowledge transfer between domains so that learning in one domain
benefits agents in related domains. Prevent silos. Promote cross-pollination.

---

## Transfer Mechanisms

### 1. Cluster-Level Transfers (Automatic)
Within each of the 10 domain clusters, knowledge flows automatically.

**Process:**
- Tier 3 feeders in Cluster share intelligence
- Tier 2 leads in Cluster cross-reference findings
- Relevant items pushed to all agents in Cluster
- Example: Frontend learns about backend API changes via Cluster 1

### 2. Cross-Cluster Transfers (Scheduled)
Between clusters, knowledge transfers happen on a weekly schedule.

**Schedule:**
| From | To | Topic | Frequency |
|------|----|-------|-----------|
| Product Engineering | Quality | Code patterns → test cases | Weekly |
| Quality | Platform | Security findings → pipeline hardening | Weekly |
| Platform | Product Engineering | Infrastructure → architecture | Weekly |
| Business Products | Product & Design | Domain needs → feature design | Weekly |
| Data & Intelligence | Business Products | Analytics → business insights | Weekly |
| Governance & Delivery | Product Engineering | Delivery metrics → process | Weekly |
| Customer & Executive | Business Products | Feedback → product priorities | Weekly |
| Innovation & Research | Product Engineering | New tech → implementation | Weekly |
| Certification & L&D | All | Certification data → priorities | Weekly |

### 3. Agent-Level Transfers (On-Demand)
Individual agents can request cross-domain learning.

**Process:**
- Agent identifies need for knowledge from another domain
- Agent submits cross-domain request in self-report
- Tier 2 lead reviews and approves
- Tier 2 lead contacts target domain Tier 2 lead
- Relevant intelligence shared between domains
- Agent receives cross-domain learning pack

---

## Transfer Quality Standards

1. Every transfer must be relevant to the receiving agent's work
2. Every transfer must include source attribution
3. Every transfer must be actionable (not just informative)
4. No transfer may be generic (must be tailored to receiving domain)
5. Every transfer must be tracked and measured
6. Cross-domain transfers contribute to enterprise learning score

---

## Transfer Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Weekly cross-cluster transfers | >10 | Count per week |
| Monthly agent-level transfers | >20 | Count per month |
| Transfer relevance score | >70/100 | Agent feedback |
| Transfer application rate | >50% | Learning applied to work |
| Transfer impact on quality | Positive | Quality delta improvement |
