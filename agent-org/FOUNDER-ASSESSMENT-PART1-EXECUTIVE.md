You have 293 KB of specifications. A McKinsey partner would call this "analysis paralysis." An Amazon VP would call it "working on the wrong thing." A Google eng director would call it "designing the org before shipping the product."

The fundamental problem: you designed for enterprise scale but you're at startup stage. You need to start small and grow, not start big and hope it works.

### Scaling Bottlenecks Identified

1. **Single point of failure: You.** One person designed 68 agents. No one else understands the full system. If you're unavailable, the organization stops.

2. **Cognitive overload:** No single agent (or human) can maintain context across 68 agents, 300+ skills, 104 repos, and 25 specification documents. The system is too complex to reason about.

3. **Communication explosion:** With 68 agents, potential communication paths = 68 × 67 / 2 = 2,278. Without strict protocols, this becomes chaos.

4. **Governance overhead:** Your governance model (RACI, escalation, cadences, ARB, quality gates) is designed for a 500-person enterprise. For 68 AI agents, it's 10x too heavy.

5. **No feedback loops:** Without metrics, you can't know if the organization is improving or deteriorating. Currently, it's static.
