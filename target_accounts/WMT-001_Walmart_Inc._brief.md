# Walmart Inc. — SAP Consulting Target Account Brief

**Industry:** Retail / E-commerce  
**HQ:** Bentonville, Arkansas, USA (702 SW 8th St)  
**Revenue:** $681B (FY2025), surpassed $700B annual sales in FY2026  
**Net Income:** $20.2B (FY2025)  
**Employees:** 2,100,000  
**CEO:** John Furner (Walmart U.S. CEO; Doug McMillon is Group CEO)  
**CIO/CDO:** David Glick (SVP & CTO — leads tech architecture); Sravana Karnati (SVP, Technology & Data — Walmart International IT)  

## ERP Stack
Custom-built retail systems for core operations, SAP for finance/supply chain back-end. Heavy investment in proprietary tech stack rather than vanilla SAP. Has been migrating workloads to cloud-native architectures.

## Cloud Strategy
Walmart Cloud (internal) + Google Cloud (e-commerce) + Azure (productivity)

## Key IT Initiatives
- Supply chain automation — automated distribution centers using Symbotic
- GenAI-powered shopping assistant and product design
- Agentic AI for retail operations (2025 Retail Rewired Report)
- Global supply chain playbook rollout (July 2025)

## Pain Points
- 2.1M employees creates massive HR/payroll complexity
- Regulatory pressure on labor practices and wages
- Global supply chain disruption resilience
- Integrating acquisitions (Vizio, Flipkart) into tech stack
- Security at massive scale (most visited retail site globally)

## Opportunity Assessment
MODERATE — Walmart is not a traditional SAP shop. They run heavily custom retail tech. Finance/supply chain SAP back-end is a potential entry point. Better angle: industry-specific consulting on supply chain AI, automation.
