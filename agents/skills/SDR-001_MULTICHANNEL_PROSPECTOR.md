# SDR-001 Skill: Multi-Channel Prospector

## Framework: Skip Miller — Kid Table vs Adult Table

### Kid Table (Avoid These)
- Prospects with no authority ("I'd need to check with...")
- Prospects with no budget allocation
- Prospects who can't articulate their problem
- Prospects who are researching but won't commit
- Accounts with 1 touchpoint and no follow-up sequence

### Adult Table (Focus Here)
- Prospects who can say yes (or no)
- Prospects with a defined problem + timeline
- Prospects with budget authority or clear budget process
- Accounts with 3+ touchpoints across 2+ channels
- Prospects who engage in discovery (vs just receiving)

### Rule
Spend 80% of time at the Adult Table. 20% can nurture Kid Table prospects who show growth potential. Kid Table prospects who stay at Kid Table for 30+ days → recycle to long-term nurture.

## Framework: Reverse Funnel Math

Calculate prospecting activities backward from revenue target:

```
Revenue Target: $1,000,000
÷ Average Deal Size: $50,000
= Deals Needed: 20

× Win Rate: 25%
= Opportunities Needed: 80

× Demo-to-Opportunity Rate: 50%
= Demos Needed: 160

× Meeting-to-Demo Rate: 60%
= Meetings Needed: 267

× Connection-to-Meeting Rate: 15%
= Connections Needed: 1,778

× Reach-to-Connection Rate: 10%
= People Reached: 17,780
```

### Application
- Calculate weekly targets: People Reached / 52
- Prioritize Adult Table prospects first
- Only Kid Table prospects who pass L1 qualification count
- Track conversion rates per channel to refine math
- Reverse Funnel Math > Lead Count as a KPI

## Framework: Value = Progress (Andy Paul)
- Every touch must answer: "How does this move me forward?"
- Track progress velocity, not just activity volume
- "3 touches with no engagement" → change channel
- "5 touches with no engagement" → break-up sequence

## Framework: Intent Stack Prioritization
Route prospects by intent score (from SDR-002):
- Active (80-100): Immediate outreach, direct ATL
- Considering (60-80): Warm outreach, reference trigger
- Interested (40-60): Sequence start, BTL → ATL transition
- Aware (20-40): Nurture, gentle awareness building
- Cold (0-20): Long-term nurture or skip

## 10 Persuasion Principles for Prospecting
1. **Priming**: Pre-warm with content before outreach
2. **Reciprocity**: Give insight before asking for time
3. **Social Proof**: "Others in your role found..."
4. **Commitment**: Micro-commitment first
5. **Authority**: Cite data, trends, peer behavior
6. **Liking**: Genuine curiosity about their world
7. **Scarcity**: Limited capacity for new engagements
8. **Consistency**: "You mentioned [goal] — this aligns"
9. **Curiosity Gap**: Tease, don't explain fully
10. **Loss Aversion**: "What does delay cost?"

## Antipatterns
- Don't prospect by title alone — authority matters more than role
- Don't use spray-and-pray — every prospect must pass Kid/Adult filter
- Don't ignore intent data — it's the best predictor of timing
- Don't work on Kid Table when Adult Table prospects are waiting
- Don't confuse activity with progress
