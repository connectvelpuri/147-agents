# MO-003 Skill: Objection Detector

## Framework: Three-Bucket Matrix (Gal Borenstein)

### BUCKET 1: "Is this SAFE?"
The buyer fears: "Will this get me fired / cause problems I'll be blamed for?"

| Objection | Translation | Response Strategy |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| "We're happy with current solution" | "Changing is risky" | Validate + reframe: "What if the risk is staying put?" |
| "I need to run this by legal/compliance" | "I need permission" | Offer case studies from regulated companies |
| "We've tried this before" | "I'm afraid of repeating failure" | "What was different about that situation?" |
| "Send me some information" | "I need to de-risk before engaging" | "I'd rather show you in 5 minutes — what's the #1 concern?" |
| "It's not a priority right now" | "I can't justify the risk" | "What would have to change for it to become a priority?" |

### BUCKET 2: "Is this the BEST choice?"
The buyer fears: "Am I making the right decision among options?"

| Objection | Translation | Response Strategy |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| "How are you different from X?" | "Help me compare" | "Great question — what criteria matter most to you?" |
| "We're evaluating multiple vendors" | "Help me choose" | "What would the ideal solution look like?" |
| "Can you share pricing?" | "Am I getting fair value?" | "Pricing depends on scope — what problem are you solving?" |
| "We need to see a comparison" | "Give me ammunition" | "Happy to — what's most important to your team?" |
| "I want to talk to references" | "Prove it works" | "Absolutely — what industry/use case would be most relevant?" |

### BUCKET 3: "Am I too early / too late?"
The buyer fears: "Is the timing wrong?"

| Objection | Translation | Response Strategy |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| "We're not ready yet" | "I don't have urgency" | "What would 'ready' look like? Let's build a timeline." |
| "We just signed a contract with X" | "I'm locked in" | "Understood. What would a successful first quarter look like?" |
| "It's budget season" | "Money is frozen" | "When does budget open? Let's align now for Q1." |
| "Too early for us" | "I don't see the need" | "What metrics would tell you it's time?" |
| "We're too small/too big" | "I don't think we fit" | "Our sweet spot is [size] — let's see if there's alignment." |

## Framework: Jeff Shore CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Technique)
Three-step process for any objection:
1. **VALIDATE**: "That makes sense given what you know"
2. **REFRAVE**: "What if the risk is actually staying put?"
3. **REPLACE**: "Here's what other leaders in your position found"

## Framework: 5 Fears (Andy Paul)

### Fear 1: Fear of Mistake
- Response: "What's the worst case if you explore this? A 15-minute call?"
- Trigger: Risk reversal, limited commitment framing

### Fear 2: Fear of Change
- Response: "The current setup works. What if it could work 2x better?"
- Trigger: Paint status quo as the real risk (loss aversion)

### Fear 3: Fear of Losing Credibility
- Response: "Your team already trusts you. Finding a better path only builds that."
- Trigger: Social proof from peers

### Fear 4: Fear of Commitment
- Response: "Let's define what 'investigating' looks like — no long-term commitment."
- Trigger: Small next step, no contract needed

### Fear 5: Fear of the Unknown
- Response: "What questions do you need answered to feel confident?"
- Trigger: Transparency, over-communicate

## Neuroscience Principles for Objection Detection
1. **Loss Aversion**: Buyers feel pain of potential loss 2x more than gain
2. **Status Quo Bias**: Inertia is the strongest competitor
3. **Reactance**: Pressure triggers resistance — give autonomy
4. **Social Proof**: "Who else is doing this?" validates safety
5. **Anchoring**: First number or claim sets the frame
6. **Cognitive Dissonance**: They rationalize staying put — you must create productive discomfort
7. **Zeigarnik Effect**: Open loops demand closure — leave questions unanswered
8. **Reciprocity**: Concessions trigger return concessions
9. **Commitment Consistency**: Past small yeses predict future big yeses
10. **Scarcity**: Limited-time/availability triggers FOMO
