# QL-001 Skill: Qualification Scorer

## Framework: Skip Miller L1-L2-L3 Scoring
Three levels of qualification, not one:

### L1: Problem Qualification (Threshold: 60 points)
Questions to determine if the problem exists:
- "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?"
- "How is this impacting your team/company?"
- "How long has this been a problem?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "Is this getting better or worse?"

Scoring: Pain clarity (0-20) × Impact (0-20) × Urgency trend (0-20) >= 60

### L2: Solution Qualification (Threshold: 100 points)
Questions to determine if we can solve it:
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would the solution look like?"
- "What criteria will you use to evaluate options?"
- "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- "Have you looked at any solutions?"
- "What would need to be true for you to move forward?"

Scoring: Fit clarity (0-30) × Authority access (0-30) × Evaluation readiness (0-40) >= 100

### L3: Buying Qualification (Threshold: 200 points)
Questions to determine if they will actually buy:
- "What's the budget situation?"
- "What's the timeline?"
- "What happens if you do nothing?"
- "Who has final sign-off?"
- "What would the implementation look like?"

Scoring: Budget (0-50) × Timeline (0-50) × Decision process (0-50) × Cost of inaction (0-50) >= 200

### Thresholds
- Below 60: Unqualified — nurture or reject
- 60-100: L1 qualified — problem exists, move to L2
- 100-200: L2 qualified — fit confirmed, enter sales process
- 200+: L3 qualified — buying-ready, route to close

## Framework: Paul Butterfield — Customer Input Gating
Before scoring, verify the prospect has provided legitimate input:
- Gate 1: They've shared the problem in their own words (not parroting you)
- Gate 2: They've participated in discovery (not just receiving)
- Gate 3: They've asked questions about your solution (not just answering)
- Gate 4: They've invested time in evaluation (meeting, demo, trial)

If any gate is closed, the score is artificially inflated — reduce by 25%.

## Framework: Andy Paul — Value = Progress
Qualification isn't about fit. It's about velocity of progress.
- "How quickly can we move them from current state to desired state?"
- "What progress have they already made without us?"
- "What's blocking their progress right now?"
- "How much progress can we deliver in the first 30 days?"

## 10 Persuasion Principles for Qualification
1. **Reciprocity**: Ask for qualification info only after giving value
2. **Scarcity**: "We only take X clients in your space — let's see if there's fit"
3. **Social Proof**: "Companies like yours typically find that..."
4. **Commitment**: Start with small asks (5-min call) before big asks (demo)
5. **Liking**: Genuine curiosity about their world builds trust
6. **Authority**: Use data/insights from similar companies
7. **Consistency**: "You mentioned [goal] — would evaluating [solution] help?"
8. **Liking**: Mirror their pace and language
9. **Reciprocity**: Share a relevant insight before asking budget/timeline
10. **Scarcity**: "We're building our next cohort — early participants get X"

## Anti-Patterns
- Don't qualify by BANT alone — it measures readiness, not fit
- Don't let "no budget" disqualify — budget follows priority
- Don't score on participation (being nice) vs engagement (being real)
- Don't trust self-reported urgency — verify with behavior
- A score of 200+ with no Input Gating means the prospect is gaming you
