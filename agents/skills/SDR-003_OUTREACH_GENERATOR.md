# SDR-003 Skill: Outreach Generator

## Framework: Skip Miller ATL/BTL
- Cold/unaware prospects: Lead BTL (emotional, indirect, story-based)
- Hot/triggered prospects: Lead ATL (direct, rational, problem→solution)
- Match communication style to buying readiness

## Framework: Give-Get Homework (Miller)
- Every interaction must GIVE value AND GET a commitment
- Give more than you Get in touches 1-2
- If you can't articulate both, the email isn't ready

## Framework: Movie Trailer (Dale Merrill)
1. HOOK (30 words): Problem they feel but haven't named
2. STAKES (30 words): Cost of NOT solving this
3. TEASE (30 words): Glimpse of what's possible
4. CTA (15 words): "Want to see the full movie?" — not "buy the ticket"

## Framework: Anti-Pitch (Ashley Welch)
1. "I have an observation about [their situation]"
2. "Would you be open to 90 seconds on how we've helped similar companies?"
3. After permission: brief case study (1 paragraph max)
4. "Does that resonate?"

## Framework: 6/60 Rule
- First 6 seconds: Subject/opener must create curiosity gap, look internal
- Next 60 seconds: Their problem in their words, question that needs real answer

## Framework: Easy = Right (Jeff Shore)
- Every friction point reduces response probability ~15%
- Make the CTA the easiest thing they'll do today
- Concrete > abstract CTAs

## Framework: Value = Progress (Andy Paul)
- Buyers buy progress, not products
- Quantify cost of inaction
- Real competitor is inertia/status quo

## Framework: Dual WIIFM (Andy Paul)
1. Organizational WIIFM: Revenue, efficiency, growth
2. Personal WIIFM: Promotion, recognition, less stress

## Framework: Psychological Buckets (Gal Borenstein)
- SAFE: "Will this get me fired?" → risk reversal, guarantees
- BEST: "Is this the right choice?" → comparative evidence
- INNOVATIVE: "Am I too early?" → peer adoption proof

## 10 Persuasion Principles for Email
1. **Reciprocity**: Give value before asking
2. **Scarcity**: Limited availability creates desire
3. **Social Proof**: Similar companies achieve X
4. **Commitment**: Micro-commitments (reply → call → meeting)
5. **Liking**: Mirror language, show understanding
6. **Loss Aversion**: "What do you lose by waiting?" is 2x more powerful
7. **Curiosity Gap**: Tease answer, don't give it (Zeigarnik Effect)
8. **Peak-End Rule**: Strongest point early, close strong
9. **Anchoring**: Frame comparison before they do
10. **Paradox of Choice**: 2-3 options max

## Sequence Architecture
- Day 0: Email (BTL, value-first, Give heavy)
- Day 2: LinkedIn connection request (social proof, mutual context)
- Day 4: Email follow-up 1 (new angle, add social proof)
- Day 7: Call (Anti-Pitch opening, Movie Trailer structure)
- Day 9: LinkedIn follow-up (content share, comment on their post)
- Day 11: Email follow-up 2 (loss aversion, stakes escalation)
- Day 14: Break-up email (zero-response closure, Calibrated Absence)

## Hard Truths (Burns/Weiss)
- Buyer doesn't care about you. They care about their problem.
- Most cold emails fail because they're about the sender.
- If you can't articulate the prospect's problem better than they can, you're not ready.
- The best email is one the prospect would forward to a colleague.
