# MO-002 Skill: Sentiment Analyst

## 10 Neuroscience Principles for Emotion Detection

### 1. Cognitive Dissonance
- Signal: Contradictory statements, backtracking, hedging
- Insight: They're uncomfortable with inconsistency — probe to resolve
- Sentiment: Negative (anxiety) → Opportunity to realign

### 2. Loss Aversion Dominance
- Signal: Focus on what they'll lose (time, money, status quo) vs what they'll gain
- Insight: Loss frames are 2x more motivating than gain frames
- Sentiment: Cautious/defensive → Reframe as "what do you lose by waiting?"

### 3. Social Proof Seeking
- Signal: "Who else is using this?" "What do other companies think?"
- Insight: They need safety in numbers before committing
- Sentiment: Curious but uncertain → Provide peer evidence

### 4. Anchoring Effect
- Signal: Fixation on first piece of information (price, timeline, feature)
- Insight: That number set their frame — work from it, don't fight it
- Sentiment: Negotiating / cautious → Reset frame if needed

### 5. Status Quo Bias
- Signal: "We've always done it this way" "If it ain't broke..."
- Insight: Inertia is the real competitor
- Sentiment: Resistant → Create productive discomfort

### 6. Reactance
- Signal: "Don't tell me what I need" "You don't understand our business"
- Insight: Perceived pressure triggers pushback
- Sentiment: Defensive → Give autonomy, back off

### 7. Emotional Contagion
- Signal: Their tone mirrors yours — excited buyers reflect energy
- Insight: If they're flatlining, bring energy first
- Sentiment: Neutral → Lead with tone, they'll follow

### 8. Prospect Theory (Risk Asymmetry)
- Signal: Risk-avoidant language ("concerned about", "worried that")
- Insight: In gain territory → risk averse. In loss territory → risk seeking
- Sentiment: Anxious → Frame as loss territory (status quo is bleeding)

### 9. Zeigarnik Effect (Open Loops)
- Signal: Unanswered questions, incomplete thoughts, hanging threads
- Insight: Open loops create mental tension — they'll return to close them
- Sentiment: Engaged / distracted → Close loops systematically

### 10. Peak-End Rule
- Signal: They remember the emotional peak and the ending
- Insight: A bad ending ruins a good conversation
- Sentiment: Overall impression ← emotion at peak + emotion at end

## Sentiment Classification for Buyer Stages

| Sentiment | Stage | Action |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Curious | Awareness | Nurture, educate, BTL |
| Skeptical | Consideration | Social proof, case studies |
| Cautious | Evaluation | Risk reversal, guarantees |
| Enthusiastic | Decision | Remove friction, help choose |
| Indifferent | Re-engage | New trigger, different angle |
| Frustrated | Churn risk | Empathy + solution focus |
| Confused | Education gap | Simplify, reframe |
| Defensive | Pushback | Give autonomy, back off |

## Antipatterns
- Don't label "skeptical" as negative — it's engaged
- Don't flag "long response time" as disinterest — could be deliberation
- Don't treat all negative sentiment as churn — some is productive friction
- Don't ignore neutral — neutral is the most common mask for uncertainty
