# SDR-002 Skill: Intent Monitor

## Signal Classification: ATL vs BTL (Skip Miller)

### ATL Signals (Conscious, Rational, Direct)
- Explicit research: visiting pricing pages, case studies, comparisons
- Job postings: hiring for roles that your product supports
- Vendor evaluation: RFP, security review requests
- Direct search: branded search for your company
- Demo requests: explicit interest signals

### BTL Signals (Subconscious, Emotional, Indirect)
- Content consumption: blog reads, social follows, newsletter subscribes
- Hiring patterns: expanding the team that would use your product
- Funding events: raised capital → likely evaluating tooling
- Leadership changes: new VP → likely to shake up tech stack
- Competitor churn signals: leaving a competitor's product
- Industry regulatory changes: forced to evaluate new approaches

### Boost Factors (3x weight)
- MULTI-PERSON: 2+ people at same company showing intent
- MULTI-CHANNEL: Same person showing intent on 2+ channels
- ROLE ALIGNMENT: Intent comes from buyer/decision-maker role
- SPEED: Multiple signals in <30 days

### Intent Scoring (Intent Stack)
- Cold: 0-20 (no signals, BTL only)
- Aware: 20-40 (1-2 BTL signals)
- Interested: 40-60 (ATL signal or 3+ BTL signals)
- Considering: 60-80 (ATL + BTL, multi-touch)
- Active: 80-100 (direct inquiry, demo request)

### Routing Rules
- Active (80-100) → SDR-001 immediately, skip SDR-003
- Considering (60-80) → SDR-003 with ATL messaging
- Interested (40-60) → SDR-003 with BTL → ATL sequencing
- Aware (20-40) → SDR-003 with nurture sequence
- Cold (0-20) → Queue for future monitoring

## 10 Neuroscience Principles for Intent Detection
1. **Cognitive Dissonance**: People act to resolve inconsistency — sudden research spikes signal tension
2. **Availability Heuristic**: Recent triggers (news, funding) inflate perceived importance
3. **Anchoring Bias**: First signal matters most — initial interaction sets frame
4. **Confirmation Bias**: Once evaluating, they seek confirming evidence — track their content diet
5. **Social Proof Threshold**: 3+ industry peers adopting = tipping point
6. **Scarcity Perception**: Time-limited triggers (trial ending, price change) accelerate action
7. **Loss Aversion**: 2x weight on "losing" signals (competitor wins, budget cuts) vs "gaining"
8. **Commitment Consistency**: Prior engagement (webinar, content download) predicts future action
9. **Salience Bias**: Recent funding, C-suite change, IPO = windows of heightened intent
10. **Status Quo Bias**: Inertia is default — intent signals must exceed activation threshold
