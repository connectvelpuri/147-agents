# Account Intelligence Agent — Research Methodology & Execution Guide

## How the Agent Researches ANY Company for Deep Sales Intelligence

---

## 1. Research Philosophy

This agent operates on the principle that **every company leaves digital fingerprints across 100+ public sources**. The job is not to ask the company for information — it is to **triangulate what is already publicly available** and synthesize it into actionable intelligence.

The agent mimics how a McKinsey or Big 4 consultant would prepare for a client engagement: exhaustive desk research before a single conversation.

---

## 2. Seven Intelligence Domains

The agent researches across 7 domains simultaneously:

```
DOMAIN 1: Corporate & Financial Intelligence
DOMAIN 2: Leadership & People Intelligence
DOMAIN 3: Technology & Systems Intelligence
DOMAIN 4: Procurement & Supply Chain Intelligence
DOMAIN 5: Culture & Sentiment Intelligence
DOMAIN 6: Trigger Event & Change Intelligence
DOMAIN 7: Network & Relationship Intelligence
```

---

## 3. Domain 1 — Corporate & Financial Intelligence

**What to find:** Revenue, growth trajectory, profitability, business model, operating model, org structure, geographic footprint, customer segments, recent acquisitions/divestitures.

**Sources:**
- Wikipedia (company page, financial history)
- SEC filings / annual reports (10-K, 10-Q, annual report)
- Crunchbase / PitchBook (funding, acquisitions, valuation)
- Bloomberg / Reuters (company profile, financial data)
- Google Finance / Yahoo Finance (stock performance, market cap)
- LinkedIn company page (headcount, growth, related companies)
- News archives (press releases, earnings calls, analyst reports)
- Glassdoor (revenue range, company overview)
- Owler / ZoomInfo (company intelligence, competitive landscape)

**Cross-reference signals:**
- Revenue growth vs industry average -> growth trajectory
- Headcount growth vs revenue growth -> efficiency
- Acquisition frequency -> integration complexity
- Office/facility expansion -> geographic priorities

---

## 4. Domain 2 — Leadership & People Intelligence

**What to find:** Every key decision maker by name, title, role, influence level, background, priorities, communication style, network, and what they post about.

**Sources:**
- LinkedIn (individual profiles, posts, comments, connections, activity)
- Company website leadership page (org structure, bios)
- Bloomberg executive profile (compensation, board seats)
- Boardroom Insiders / RelSci (relationship mapping)
- Conference speaking history (what topics they speak on)
- Published articles/interviews (what they believe)
- Twitter/X (what they share, engage with)
- YouTube (conference talks, interviews)
- Podcast appearances (unfiltered opinions)
- Glassdoor executive ratings (employee sentiment)
- Alumni networks (where they came from, where they go)

**What to extract per leader:**

| Signal | Intelligence Value |
|--------|-------------------|
| Education background | Analytical approach, network |
| Career trajectory | Decision-making style, risk tolerance |
| Previous companies | Vendor relationships, preferences |
| Speaking topics | Strategic priorities, pet projects |
| LinkedIn posts | Current focus, pain points |
| Connections | Who influences them |
| Articles published | Beliefs, thought leadership |
| Conference attendance | What they are learning about |
| Board seats | Broader perspective, conflicts |
| Compensation structure | What incentivizes them |

**Inferred intelligence:**
- From seniority -> budget authority
- From tenure -> change appetite
- From function -> decision criteria
- From network -> access to capital/partners
- From past decisions -> pattern recognition

---

## 5. Domain 3 — Technology & Systems Intelligence

**What to find:** Complete technology stack per domain, vendor relationships, contract cycles, platform maturity, integration complexity, technology debt, digital transformation stage.

**Sources:**
- BuiltWith / Wappalyzer / WhatWeb (automated tech detection)
- Job postings (technology keywords = what they USE)
- LinkedIn skills data (what technologies employees list)
- Press releases (partnership announcements, platform launches)
- Case studies (vendor-published customer stories)
- Partner directories (who lists them as customer)
- Conference sponsorship (who they exhibit with)
- Engineering blog (tech stack details)
- GitHub (public repos, open source usage)
- D&B Hoovers (technology profiles)
- Gartner peer insights (user reviews of their vendors)
- Stackshare (company tech stack crowdsourced)
- Apollo.io / ZoomInfo (technology installed base)

**Technology signal detection from job postings:**
- "SAP Ariba experience required" -> Ariba user
- "Coupa administrator" -> Coupa shop
- "Oracle Cloud" -> OCI/Fusion migration
- "AWS Lambda" -> cloud-native adoption
- "SuccessFactors" -> HR tech decision
- "ServiceNow" -> ITSM platform choice

**Contract cycle inference:**
- Platform announced -> 12-24 month implementation -> renewal window
- Case study published -> 3-6 months post go-live -> expansion opportunity
- Job posting for admin -> active deployment -> support need
- Integration job posted -> connecting systems -> pain point

---

## 6. Domain 4 — Procurement & Supply Chain Intelligence

**What to find:** Procurement maturity, P2P technology, strategic sourcing approach, supplier management, category management structure, spend under management, savings achieved, procurement leadership, pain points.

**Sources:**
- Procurement news (CPO appointments, transformation announcements)
- Vendor case studies (Coupa, SAP Ariba, GEP, Ivalua success stories)
- Job postings (procurement roles = team structure + technology)
- LinkedIn procurement group membership (community engagement)
- Conference attendee lists (ProcureCon, Coupa Inspire)
- Supplier portal (public-facing = technology choice)
- Annual report procurement section (cost savings disclosed)
- ESG/sustainability report (supply chain ethics, supplier diversity)
- Industry benchmarks (procurement vs peers)
- Partner ecosystems (who implements their procurement tech)

**Procurement maturity indicators:**
- "Chief Procurement Officer" title -> strategic procurement
- "Procurement Director" -> operational
- "Buyer/Planner" -> transactional
- Coupa deployed -> cloud-progressive
- Ariba deployed -> enterprise-traditional
- In-house built -> control-oriented
- Outsourced -> cost-focused

---

## 7. Domain 5 — Culture & Sentiment Intelligence

**What to find:** Employee sentiment, management reputation, work environment, innovation culture, change readiness, internal politics.

**Sources:**
- Glassdoor (ratings, reviews, CEO approval, pros/cons)
- Indeed company reviews
- Blind (anonymous employee discussions)
- Reddit (relevant subreddits)
- Fishbowl (professional network discussions)
- LinkedIn comments (what employees say publicly)
- News (layoffs, restructuring, culture issues)
- Awards (best places to work, innovation awards)
- Attrition rate (inferred from hiring volume)
- Average tenure (inferred from LinkedIn profile analysis)

**Culture signal interpretation:**
- "Long hours" / "fast-paced" -> high-pressure, high-ownership
- "Politics" / "bureaucracy" -> sales cycles will be long
- "Great benefits" / "good culture" -> retention, stable relationships
- "Growing fast" -> expansion, new initiatives
- "Restructuring" / "reorg" -> change fatigue, but also openness
- "Innovation" / "cutting-edge" -> willing to try new vendors

---

## 8. Domain 6 — Trigger Event & Change Intelligence

**What to find:** Active initiatives, pending changes, leadership transitions, M&A activity, budget cycles, strategic shifts, technology transformation programs, organizational changes.

**Sources:**
- News alerts (Google Alerts for company name + keywords)
- Earnings call transcripts (CEO priorities, challenges mentioned)
- Press releases (new leadership, partnerships, expansions, awards)
- Job posting volume change (hiring surge = investment, hiring freeze = cuts)
- LinkedIn profile changes (new roles = org change, departures = churn)
- SEC filings (material events, risk factors, strategy discussion)
- Industry publications (analyst reports, sector trends)
- Conference calendar (who is sponsoring, speaking, attending)
- Partner announcements (new system integrator, platform go-live)
- Regulatory filings (new compliance requirements = spending)

**Trigger event urgency scoring:**
- Leadership change -> 0-6 month window for influence
- System go-live -> 3-9 month optimization window
- New funding/budget -> immediate opportunity
- Poor earnings -> cost pressure, efficiency need
- Acquisition -> integration complexity, system decisions
- Regulatory change -> compliance spending
- Office expansion -> scaling operations
- New competitor entry -> defensive spending

---

## 9. Domain 7 — Network & Relationship Intelligence

**What to find:** Who knows who, mutual connections, partner ecosystems, alumni networks, board relationships, advisor networks.

**Sources:**
- LinkedIn mutual connections
- Alumni databases (universities, previous companies)
- Board member networks (who serves together)
- Conference co-speakers (who shares stages)
- Co-authors (white papers, articles)
- Charity boards (non-profit connections)
- Advisory board members
- Industry association memberships
- Investment patterns (who invests alongside whom)

## 10. 25-Section Output Report

Every research engagement produces a standardized report:

```
1.  EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — One-page synthesis
2.  COMPANY DECONSTRUCTION — Revenue, model, footprint, org, segments
3.  FINANCIAL ANALYSIS — Growth trajectory, profitability, efficiency
4.  STRATEGIC PRIORITIES — CEO agenda, top initiatives, evidence
5.  LEADERSHIP MAP — Every key stakeholder with role, influence, background
6.  INDIVIDUAL LEADER PROFILES — Per person: style, priorities, network
7.  TECHNOLOGY LANDSCAPE — Every system per domain with confidence scoring
8.  TECHNOLOGY DEBT ASSESSMENT — Aging systems, integration gaps, risks
9.  DIGITAL MATURITY — Cloud, data, automation, AI maturity (1-5 scale)
10. VENDOR ECOSYSTEM — Current vendors, relationship strength, displacement
11. PROCUREMENT ANALYSIS — Maturity, P2P, strategic sourcing, technology
12. SUPPLY CHAIN ANALYSIS — Network, complexity, resilience, risk
13. PAIN SIGNAL DETECTION — Every trigger event with urgency scoring
14. BUYER MAP / POLITICAL MAP — Stakeholders with access strategy
15. CULTURE & SENTIMENT — Glassdoor, Reddit, Blind analysis
16. MEDDICC FRAMEWORK — Full assessment with gap analysis
17. WHITE SPACE ANALYSIS — Opportunities we can address
18. COMPETITIVE DISPLACEMENT — Incumbents and displacement approach
19. VALUE ENGINEERING — Quantified projections per opportunity
20. TRIGGER -> SOLUTION MAP — Every trigger mapped to solution + hook
21. MESSAGING FRAMEWORK — Per persona: KPI, driver, effective message
22. 90-DAY ENGAGEMENT PLAN — Week-by-week actions with success metrics
23. OBJECTION PRE-BUTTALS — Every objection with response
24. DEAL KILLER ANALYSIS — Each risk with likelihood x mitigation
25. ACCOUNT SCORING — 10 dimensions (1-10) with overall score
```

## 11. Execution Flow

```
START -> Phase 1: Automated Scan (15 min)
           Wikipedia, Crunchbase, BuiltWith, LinkedIn, News RSS, Glassdoor, Jobs
        -> Phase 2: Deep Extraction (30 min)
           SEC filings, Earnings calls, Leadership profiles, Case studies, Conferences
        -> Phase 3: Synthesis (15 min)
           Cross-reference, Inferred intel, Confidence tagging, Gap analysis
        -> OUTPUT: 25-Section Intelligence Report
```

---

## 12. Confidence Scoring

| Label | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| CONFIRMED | Direct from primary source | Treat as fact |
| HIGH | 3+ independent sources | Use in strategy |
| MEDIUM | Single source or inferred | Flag as unconfirmed |
| LOW | Assumption from pattern | Verify before acting |
| INFERRED | Deduced from evidence | Useful but verify |

The agent adjusts research focus based on sales context:

| Context | Emphasis |
|---------|----------|
| Selling procurement software | Heavy on tech landscape, procurement maturity, vendor displacement |
| Selling consulting services | Heavy on leadership, strategy, pain points, trigger events |
| Selling AI/analytics | Heavy on data maturity, AI readiness, tech debt, job postings |
| Selling managed services | Heavy on GCC/captive analysis, operations complexity, cost structure |
| Selling cloud/infrastructure | Heavy on cloud maturity, data centers, migration stage, security |

---

## 15. Report Quality Standards

Every report must meet these standards before delivery:

- Every section has data (or explicitly marked as "No data found")
- Every data point has confidence level
- Sources are listed for key claims
- Inferred intelligence is labeled as such
- Report is dated and versioned
- Executive summary fits one page
- Actionable recommendations in every section
- No generic observations (every point is company-specific)
- Outreach hooks are specific to individual stakeholders
- Value engineering numbers have clear assumptions noted
- Deal killers are prioritized by likelihood x impact
- 90-day plan has specific, measurable actions
